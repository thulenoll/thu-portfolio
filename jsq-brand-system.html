JSQ brand design system in Claude Design
01 — The system

Built in Figma, from the tokens up.

I built Juniper Square's brand design system in Figma — color, spacing, and typography tokens and styles. Because slide presentations are such a common medium at the company, I split it into two variable systems: one tuned for web, one for slide-deck design.

Alongside the Figma work, I wrote the specs the system needs to travel: JSON files for slide-deck specifications, and Markdown guidelines for usage, voice, and tone.

02 — The Claude plugin

The same system, as a plugin the company designs with.

I turned the system into a Claude plugin with a set of skills the team can call directly — published in our organization plugins in Claude.

/brand-identityColors, spacing, type, and styles
/slide-deck-designOn-brand presentations from the deck specs
/voice-and-toneHow the brand sounds in writing
/brand-qaChecks work against the guidelines

The same foundations also power our brand system inside Claude Design. I keep developing it — building social-asset and web components and bringing them into Claude Design.

03 — A closer look

A sample of the system, up close.

A comprehensive example of the work — color, type, and spacing tokens across the web and slide-deck modes, with a look at the brand guidelines. Not the whole system, but enough to show how it's built and documented.

Outcome

One brand system, in two places it's actually used — a published Claude plugin and our Claude Design brand system — so people and AI start on-brand by default.
