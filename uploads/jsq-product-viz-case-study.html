01 — Context

B2B software is hard to see.

Juniper Square builds connected software, data, and fund-administration services for the people running private markets — GPs scaling their firms, LPs tracking their investments, fund administrators reconciling the back-office. The product is genuinely complex: dense data tables, multi-step workflows, financial reporting that has to be exact.

Complex products are hard to see. A screenshot of a fund-admin workflow doesn't sell itself. The job of product visualization is to take that complexity and make it legible to people who haven't used the product yet — to show them what it would feel like before they sign up.

02 — The bridge

Between product, PMMs, and the people who buy.

My role sits between three groups: product design, who builds the actual UI; product marketing managers, who own the story of what the product does for the customer; and the marketing surfaces where prospects encounter the brand for the first time. The work is translation — taking the real product UI and abstracting it into something a prospect can read in a glance and a story they can follow in a video.

The hard part isn't making it look good. It's deciding what to leave out so the part that matters can be seen.
Notes on product visualization

A landing page hero, a storyboard for a sales video, a product visualization for a feature page — each one is a different level of abstraction from the real UI. The decisions I make are mostly subtractive: which fields to keep, which to remove, which interaction to freeze on, which to animate. The brand sits underneath all of it as the connective tissue.

03 — Surfaces

Four kinds of translation, one brand voice.

The work shows up across four kinds of surfaces — each with a different audience, a different level of abstraction, and a different role inside the funnel.

Surface 01
Web & landing pages
Product visualizations on the marketing site and campaign landing pages. The highest-traffic surface — every feature page, every campaign destination.
Surface 02
Storyboards
Frame-by-frame storyboards for marketing videos. The bridge step between PMM message and the final video — where the narrative gets drawn before it gets animated.
Surface 03
Videos
Product walkthroughs and feature explainers. Storyboards animated into the surfaces sales teams use in demos and prospects watch on the site.
Surface 04
Product UI visualizations
Abstracted product UI for use on marketing surfaces. Real interactions, simplified enough to read at a glance — without losing fidelity to what's actually in the product.
Outcome

Active and ongoing — translating product UIs across web, storyboards, videos, and the marketing site. Bridge work between product design, PMMs, and the surfaces prospects see first.
